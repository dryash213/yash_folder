Class 12 Physics Sample Paper
Section A: (1 mark each)
Define speed.
Write the SI unit of force.
State Newton's Second Law of Motion.
What is the principle of conservation of energy?
Define work done.
What is the formula for potential energy?
Section B: (2 marks each)
Calculate the weight of an object of mass 10 kg on the surface of the Earth (g = 9.8 m/s²).
Explain the difference between scalar and vector quantities.
A car accelerates from rest to a speed of 30 m/s in 15 seconds. Find its acceleration.
Write the equation of motion for uniformly accelerated motion.
A ball is thrown vertically upward. What is its velocity at the highest point?
Explain the concept of momentum and its formula.
Section C: (3 marks each)
Derive the equation of motion using the graphical method.
State and explain the laws of reflection.
Calculate the work done when a force of 10 N moves an object 5 m in the direction of the force.
Explain the concept of projectile motion with an example.
A body moves with uniform acceleration. If its initial velocity is 10 m/s and it travels 100 m in 10 seconds, find the acceleration.
Define kinetic energy and give its formula.
Explain the law of conservation of momentum with an example.
Calculate the potential energy of an object of mass 5 kg at a height of 10 m (g = 9.8 m/s²).
Discuss the motion of an object in free fall.
Explain the concept of inertia.
Section D: (4 marks each)
State and explain Bernoulli’s principle.
Derive the expression for gravitational potential energy.
A car traveling at 80 km/h applies brakes and stops in 4 seconds. Calculate the deceleration.
Explain the different types of friction.
Discuss the factors affecting the motion of an object.
Derive the equation for the height of a projectile.
Explain the concept of simple harmonic motion with examples.
A block of mass 10 kg is placed on a frictionless surface. If a force of 20 N is applied, what will be its acceleration?
